About

Hi, my name is Ibby Dalrymple and I'm a UX designer based in London.

I always knew that I wanted to work in the art world. After completing an art foundation course in Edinburgh I realised that I wanted to turn away from subjective fine art in order to focus on more objective functional design, as that which is aesthetic but also academically stimulating kickstarts my imagination. This was confirmed in me after working at various different incarnations of the art world. I therefore enrolled at GA’s immersive UX design course and have since discovered a huge love and affinity for it. I want to pursue this in a professional capacity, and my dream job would be as a UX designer in a friendly and creative cross-functional team, building something that positively impacts people’s lives.

Design Process

  • User Research

    I start by interviewing, observing and testing users to understand how they interact with the product. This allows me to create a product that fits the users’ needs.

  • Interaction & Interface Design

    I then define the way my digital product interacts with users so that it is simple, user-friendly, and beautiful. This allows me to create a product that people love to use.

  • Prototyping & Testing

    Next, I create interactive prototypes to test my designs and ideas in front of real users. I then iterate and evolve my concepts into a digital product. This allows me to create a product that actually works.

I trained at a 3 month intensive UX Design course at General Assembly. It was taught by qualified proffesionals who often had decades of experience of the industry behind them. The general format of the day would be for us to start the day with a stand up. We would then have a morning of lectures, discussions and activities. After lunch we would have workshop time where would practice our new skills, work on our projects, and receive extra help from the instructors. Over the four months we completed 5 projects. They ranged from 1- 3 weeks long and some were independent ventures whereas others were group projects.
